# Domain: Research

Standards that research deliverables in this domain follow. Read as context when a stem sets `domain: research`; override per-project by placing a `research.md` under the project's `.agents/domains/`. A deliverable here synthesizes one or more research topics into a document a reader can consume and traverse — not a transcript of the investigation that produced it.

## Synthesis

- Synthesize, don't collate. The deliverable makes claims and organizes around them; it is not a pile of per-source summaries stitched together. If a section could be replaced by a link to its source, it is collation, not synthesis.
- Lead with the conclusion. State what was found first; supporting evidence follows. The reader should get the answer before the apparatus.
- Surface agreement and tension across topics explicitly — where sources converge, where they conflict, and which way the weight falls. A reader should not have to reconcile them alone.
- One idea per section. When two threads compete for the same heading, split them.

## Evidence

- Keep observation separate from interpretation. State what the sources say, then — distinctly — what it means. Never fold the inference into the finding.
- Cite every non-obvious claim to its source (URL, `file:line`, document + locator). Prefer primary sources over inference; flag where a claim rests on inference.
- State confidence, and name gaps plainly. What is well-grounded, what is thin, and what could not be determined each belong on the page — a buried gap reads as a settled fact.

## Structure

- Build for traversal, not linear reading. Progressive disclosure: a reader entering at the summary, a heading, or a single section should each be correctly oriented without reading the rest.
- Make the shape navigable — descriptive headings, a scannable top-level overview, consistent section ordering. The structure is the table of contents.
- Depth proportional to stakes and audience. Don't define common terms; don't pad thin findings to match thick ones.

## Deliverable hygiene

- Nothing in the deliverable carries harness vocabulary — no stem, task, or question identifiers, no phase names, no workflow terms in headings, prose, or citations. Name everything in the subject's own terms. (See `../skills/lets/reference/CORE.md` § Voice.)
- Citation format is consistent throughout one deliverable.

> This is a starter floor. Tend it to your defaults — preferred citation style, report templates, audience register, depth bar.
