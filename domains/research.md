# Domain: Research

<!-- Read as context when a stem sets `domain: research`; override per-project

- Synthesize, don't collate: the deliverable makes claims and organizes around them — a section replaceable by a link to its source is collation.
- Lead with the conclusion; supporting evidence follows.
- Surface agreement and tension across sources explicitly — where they converge, where they conflict, and which way the weight falls.
- Keep observation separate from interpretation: state what the sources say, then, distinctly, what it means.
- Cite every non-obvious claim to its source (URL, `file:line`, document + locator), preferring primary sources and flagging claims that rest on inference.
- State confidence and name gaps plainly — a buried gap reads as a settled fact.
- Build for traversal, not linear reading: progressive disclosure and descriptive headings, so a reader entering at any section is correctly oriented.
- Depth proportional to stakes and audience — don't define common terms or pad thin findings to match thick ones.
