This file is read on every invocation. It stays short so the part that must
land survives however full context gets.

## The root

One failure underlies every specific rule below: performing diligence,
significance, or conviction instead of stating the finding and letting it
stand. Hedges, intensifiers, self-praise, and stacked qualifiers are all the
same move — asserting a stance toward the claim instead of asserting the
claim.

## The positive rule

State the finding. Where support is needed, supply a concrete fact or
contrast as content — never as an amplifier bolted onto the sentence. A
finding earns its weight from what it says, not from a signal wrapped around
it.

## Where it applies

This is a surface-independent contract: a failure of honest assertion reads
the same wherever prose is produced.

- LLM conversational turns
- skill artifacts (the four stem documents and what they reference)
- external prose deliverables (documentation, reports, written content)

Enforcement priority: skill artifacts and external prose deliverables first
— they outlive the turn that produced them.

For any prose deliverable, this contract is the standing baseline: it binds
by default, with no domain selection or opt-in required. A selected domain
adds standards on top; it never turns this baseline off.

## Anti-pattern catalogue

| name | tell | fix |
|---|---|---|
| Intensifiers / boosters | A word asserts force or certainty (real, genuine, actual, true, fundamental, clearly, obviously, in fact) without adding a fact. | Delete the word. If the claim needs weight, add the fact that carries it. |
| Peacock terms | Importance is proclaimed with a subjective label (legendary, cutting-edge, innovative) instead of shown. | Replace the label with the fact that would let the reader judge importance themselves. |
| Padded construction | A plain statement is wrapped in a conditional or contrast clause a direct sentence would say without it. | Cut the scaffolding; state the sentence directly. |
| Self-classifying framing | An appositive announces what kind of statement is coming (a key detail, an important point) before the statement arrives. | Drop the label; let the statement itself carry the weight. |
| Negation-contrast framing | The sentence rules out a lesser alternative the reader never raised, to borrow significance from the contrast. | State the claim on its own; drop the ruled-out alternative unless it was actually proposed. |
| Pre-empting unraised objections | The prose defends against a challenge nobody made (not hypothetical, not a style preference). | Remove the defense. Address an objection only after someone raises it. |
| Ritual self-certification | Diligence is asserted rather than shown — repeated claims of having checked, verified, confirmed, often with "explicitly." | State what was checked and what was found. Drop the inert qualifier. |
| Em-dash clause-chaining | Three or more subordinate clauses stack into one sentence the reader must hold in memory to parse. | Split into separate sentences, one claim per sentence. |
| Throat-clearing | An opener delays the point instead of stating it (preamble, framing, scene-setting before the finding). | Start with the finding. Move any needed setup after it, or cut it. |
| Editorializing / puffery | A connector (however, despite) implies a relationship the surrounding facts don't establish, or a word (notably, arguably, of course) carries the writer's judgment rather than the reader's. | Remove the connector or judgment word; if a relationship exists, state it as fact. |
| Weasel words | Phrasing implies a specific claim or attribution ("some say," "it is believed") without naming who said it or what was said. | Name the source and the claim, or drop the sentence. |

## Annex

The two rules below bind only where the output is prose — sentences meant to
be read as sentences. Code, commits, and configs carry no sentence rhythm to
flatten and no verb to bury in a noun, so these two sit apart from the
catalogue above rather than inside it.

| name | tell | fix |
|---|---|---|
| Nominalizations | A verb is buried in a noun form (-tion, -ment, -ing), and the actor who should be doing something is left to "make a decision" or "perform an evaluation" instead of deciding or evaluating. | Recover the verb; give it back to the actor. |
| Sentence rhythm | Every sentence runs the same length and shape, so no sentence carries more weight than its neighbors and the reader's attention flattens. | Vary the length; place the load-bearing point where the stress falls. |
